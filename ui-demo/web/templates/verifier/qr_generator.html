This is OID4VP — the verifier-initiated presentation flow. What just happened: the request builder asked walt.id's verifier-api to mint an OID4VP authorization request. Below you'll see the resulting openid4vp://authorize?... URL plus a QR encoding the same URL. Send the URL or QR to a holder, they open it in their wallet, the wallet picks a matching credential and POSTs a vp_token back to walt.id's response_uri, walt.id verifies the proof, and the result appears on this page automatically (polling every 3 seconds).
Different from the holder-initiated direct-share path on the holder's "Present Credential" page — that one is a platform-hosted convenience (no OID4VP, no vp_token, no verifier authorization request) where the holder pushes a credential to a one-off /share/v/<id> URL. OID4VP is the standards-compliant flow; direct-share is a pragmatic shortcut.
